Based on the frontend code, here's a complete walkthrough of the UI:

---

## Page 1 — Home Page (`/`)

**Hero Section** at the top:
- A badge: `✨ AI-Powered Analysis`
- Large headline: *"Compare Insurance Policies — Instantly & Intelligently"*
- 3 stat pills: **3 Sections Analyzed · AI Powered Engine · 100% Accurate Comparison**

**Upload Card** below it:
- Two drag-and-drop zones side by side with a **VS** divider between them
- Each zone shows: `📂 Drag & drop or click to browse` / `TXT only · max 5 MB`
- Once a file is dropped, it shows the filename + size + a `✕` remove button
- A **Compare Policies** button (disabled until both files are selected)
- A progress bar appears while uploading/processing

**How It Works** section — 3 steps in a row:
> 1. 📤 Upload Documents → 2. 🔍 AI Extraction → 3. 📊 Side-by-side View

**Feature Highlights** — 4 colored cards:
> 🛡️ Coverage Analysis · 🚫 Exclusions Mapping · 💰 Cost Breakdown · 📂 History Tracking

---

## Page 2 — Comparison Page (`/comparison/:id`)

**Top bar**: breadcrumb `← New Comparison / Comparison #5`, a status badge (green `completed`), timestamp, and a **⬇ Download PDF** button.

### Section A — Comparison View
- **Policy name headers**: `[Policy 1] filename.txt` ←→ **VS** ←→ `[Policy 2] filename.txt`
- **Summary Banner**: stat cards showing shared coverage count, unique items, exclusions count, advantages for each policy

**3 tabs** below:
| Tab | Content |
|---|---|
| 🛡️ Coverage | Table of shared items (both policies side by side) + lists of items unique to each |
| 🚫 Exclusions | Same layout for exclusions |
| 💰 Premiums | Side-by-side table: annual premium, monthly, deductible, copay, coinsurance, OOP max + plain-text differences |

### Section B — Anomaly Detection 🔍
- A **Run Scan** button
- After scan: severity filter pills (`All · Critical · Warning · Info`)
- Each anomaly card shows: severity icon (🚨⚠️ℹ️), affected policy badge, category icon, title, description, recommendation, detected by `Rule` or `AI` tag

### Section C — Interactive Charts 📊
- **Coverage Grouped Bar** — Policy 1 vs Policy 2 across Shared/Exclusive Coverage/Exclusions
- **Coverage Donut** — Shared vs Only P1 vs Only P2
- **Exclusions Donut** — same breakdown for exclusions
- **Premium Bar** — side-by-side dollar comparison of all premium fields
- **Similarity Histogram** — distribution of similarity scores among matched items

### Section D — Q&A Chatbot 💬
- Header: *"Ask About This Comparison"*
- 4 suggested question buttons to start: *"Which policy has better coverage overall?"* etc.
- Chat interface: user messages (right-aligned bubbles) + AI responses (left-aligned) with a **confidence badge** (High/Medium/Low) and **relevant section tags** (🛡️ coverage, 💰 premiums, etc.)
- Text input + **Send** button at the bottom

### Section E — Policy Recommendation 🎯
- A form with dropdowns: Age group, Budget preference, Primary health concern, Risk tolerance
- **Get Recommendation** button
- Result: a winner card with 🏆 icon, recommended policy name, confidence level, reasoning paragraph, key factors list, caveats
- Below: 4 expandable cards for standard profiles: *Young Single Professional, Family with Children, Senior/Retired, Chronic Condition Management*

### Section F — Plain-English Summary 📖
- Two expandable **policy cards** (one per policy) each showing:
  - One-liner description
  - ✅ What it covers / 🚫 What it doesn't / 💰 How much it costs
  - 👍 Biggest Strength + 👎 Biggest Weakness
- A **Comparison Panel** below with: executive summary, key difference, head-to-head winner badge (🏆 or 🤝)

---

## Page 3 — History Page (`/history`)

- Header: **Comparison History** + `+ New Comparison` button
- A table with columns: `# · Policy 1 · Policy 2 · Status · Date · Actions`
- Status shown as colored badges: green `completed`, red `failed`, yellow `processing`
- Each row has a **View** button (links to comparison page) and a **Delete** button
